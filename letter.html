A Letter for You

Take your time with this one.

S

tap the envelope to open it

Sampurna,

I've started this letter more times than I want to admit, mostly because I kept trying to make it sound perfect, and then I remembered — you never needed perfect from me. You just needed honest.

So, honestly: these last weeks without you have been quiet in a way I don't like. Not peaceful-quiet. Just empty. I keep noticing the shape of things you used to fill — the seat next to me, the other side of the phone call, the person I'd tell something silly to first.

I'm not writing this to ask you to forget what led us here, or to pretend I've got it all figured out. I know a website doesn't fix what actually needs fixing between us — that only happens if we sit down, really listen to each other, and are willing to change the things that need changing. I want to do that. I'm ready to do that, for as long as it takes.

But before any of those harder conversations, I wanted you to have this — our story, our photos, that one ticket stub from Lake Mall — all in one place, so you know exactly how I see us. Not as something broken beyond repair. As something worth trying for, one more time.

Whatever you decide, I needed you to know: without you, I am incomplete. Those memories are a lifeline I won't let get erased, even if all I can do right now is remind you they exist.

I'm here whenever you're ready to talk. No pressure, no countdown. Just me, waiting to listen properly this time.

— Yours, always

A note for you (the sender): I kept this letter honest but general on purpose — I don't know the specific thing that led to the separation, and a real apology needs real specifics. Before you publish this, consider opening the letter.html file and adding a line or two naming what you're actually sorry for, or what you're promising to do differently. That detail is what will make this land.

Made with everything I have left to give, for Sampurna.
